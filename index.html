Scalable Training Estimation and Planning System – FETP India

Policy decision aid Version 1.0

What STEPS does

STEPS helps policy makers explore different ways to scale Field Epidemiology Training Programs (FETPs) in India. It combines evidence on stakeholder preferences from a discrete choice experiment, costing information from Indian FETP partners, and simple epidemiological multipliers based on evaluations of FETP programmes in other settings.

Users can adjust key program features such as FETP tier, mentorship intensity, mode of delivery, response time expectations, number of cohorts, cohort size, and cost per trainee per month. STEPS then summarises:

  • Expected endorsement of the configuration by decision makers.
  • Programme and economic cost, with the option to include opportunity costs.
  • Simple indicators of epidemiological impact and benefit cost ratios.
  • Side by side comparison of saved scenarios for use in policy dialogues.

The tool is designed for laptops, tablets, and mobile devices so that it can be used in workshops and meetings with central and state level stakeholders.

Quick start

  1. Go to the Configuration tab and choose a programme tier, mentorship level, response time, delivery mode, number of cohorts, and cohort size.
  2. Use the slider for Cost per trainee per month to keep values inside the range used in the discrete choice experiment (₹75,000 to ₹400,000).
  3. Select the preference model: average mixed logit or the more supportive latent class group.
  4. Click Update results to see endorsement, costs, and benefits, and open the snapshot window for a concise summary.
  5. Save interesting options in the Saved scenarios tab for side by side comparison or download to Excel and PDF for reporting.

For a fuller description of the methods, including econometric models and epidemiological assumptions, see the Technical appendix tab.

Attributes and levels used in the DCE

The discrete choice experiment asked senior stakeholders to choose between alternative FETP configurations. The main attributes and levels were:

Programme tier

  • Frontline: short entry level surveillance training.
  • Intermediate: one year part time training with field projects.
  • Advanced: two year full time fellowship at national or state level.

Career incentives

  • Government and partner certificate (reference).
  • University qualification.
  • Government career pathway.

Mentorship intensity

  • Low mentorship (reference).
  • Medium mentorship.
  • High mentorship.

Mode of delivery

  • Blended (mix of in person and online, reference).
  • In person.
  • Fully online.

Outbreak response capacity

  • Respond within 30 days (reference).
  • Respond within 15 days.
  • Respond within 7 days.

Cost per trainee per month

Monthly programme cost per trainee, expressed in Indian Rupees and varied between ₹75,000 and ₹400,000 in the experiment.

In the model specification, one alternative specific constant captured any left right bias for the first training option, while a separate constant captured the general tendency to choose the opt out alternative. Only the opt out constant is used when predicting overall endorsement of any FETP option versus no programme.

Cost template and sources

Cost templates are drawn from detailed costings supplied by FETP partners in India. For Frontline training, costs are based on estimates from WHO. For Intermediate training, separate templates are provided for WHO, NIE, and NCDC. For Advanced training, templates reflect NIE and NCDC cost structures. For Intermediate and Advanced programmes, costs from different institutions are not combined; instead, they can be used as alternative cost structures.

STEPS uses these templates to allocate the total cohort cost across direct and indirect components. Opportunity cost of trainee time is estimated where this was provided in the original cost templates.

About STEPS

STEPS has been developed to support the Government of India and partners in planning the scale up of Field Epidemiology Training Programmes. It is designed as a practical decision aid that links stakeholder preferences from a discrete choice experiment with costing data and simple epidemiological assumptions.

The business case focuses on advanced FETPs, with a secondary focus on intermediate FETPs, and recognises the need for a skilled public health workforce to meet national and international commitments on health security. The tool allows decision makers to test what happens to costs, benefits, and endorsement when they alter programme design features or expand the number of cohorts.

All calculations are transparent and traceable. Users can inspect the cost templates, review the main modelling assumptions, and download a technical appendix with full methodological detail.

Prepared by Mesfin Genie, PhD, Newcastle Business School, The University of Newcastle, Australia. For questions or clarifications please contact mesfin.genie@newcastle.edu.au.
