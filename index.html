What STEPS does

STEPS combines preferences from a discrete choice experiment with simple cost and epidemiological assumptions to support high level discussions about scaling up Frontline, Intermediate and Advanced FETP options in India.

  • Predicts endorsement versus opt out for different programme designs.
  • Aggregates costs and indicative benefits for selected cohorts.
  • Summarises graduates and outbreak responses supported over a planning horizon.

Use this tool to compare configurations, not to replace full economic evaluations. All values are indicative and should be refined with country specific costing and epidemiological data.

How to use STEPS in meetings

  1. Select the programme tier, incentives, mentorship and delivery in the Configuration tab.
  2. Adjust the cost per trainee per month, cohort sizes and number of cohorts.
  3. Apply the configuration to see endorsement, costs and benefits in the Results tab.
  4. Save promising scenarios and compare them in the National simulation tab.
  5. Use the policy brief PDF and Excel export to capture key messages.

Costing templates

The cost template selected in the Configuration tab determines how the programme cost per trainee per month is allocated across major cost components and how the opportunity cost share is applied.

Where available, WHO, NIE and NCDC templates can be compared. External cost_config.json files allow direct use of detailed costing work with no change to this interface.

All cost results remain indicative. For investment cases, replace stylised assumptions with fully validated costing studies.
