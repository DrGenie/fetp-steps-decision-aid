STEPS

STEPS – Scalable Training Estimation and Planning System

Decision aid for strengthening and scaling up FETP India

NCDC • World Bank • Partners

What STEPS does

STEPS helps NCDC and partners explore scenarios for scaling up Frontline, Intermediate and Advanced FETP training in India. It combines:

  • Mixed logit preference estimates from a discrete choice experiment (DCE) of decision makers.
  • Harmonised cost templates for each tier of FETP training.
  • Epidemiological benefit assumptions (outbreak responses and non-outbreak value).
  • A simple capacity and feasibility check for mentors and training sites.

STEPS is not a budget or operational plan. It is a transparent decision aid to support discussion of scale-up options and trade-offs against available resources and capacity.

How to use this tool

  1. Review attributes & levels used in the preference study.
  2. Align settings & assumptions with your preferred values.
  3. Use the configuration tab to define one FETP scale-up option.
  4. Review results including endorsement, perceived programme value and epidemiological benefits.
  5. Check costing details and explicit mentor support costs.
  6. Use national view to interpret benefits and costs at national scale.
  7. Save scenarios, then generate a policy-ready PDF or Excel export.
  8. Use the Copilot / ChatGPT briefing tab to obtain a short policy brief or slide deck.

Attributes and levels from the DCE

The mixed logit preference model in STEPS is based on the following FETP programme attributes and levels. These should not be changed in configuration, to remain consistent with the preference study.

Attribute Levels used in the DCE How this appears in STEPS
Programme tier Frontline, Intermediate, Advanced The programme tier dropdown in the Configuration tab.
Career incentive Certificate, university qualification, government career pathway The career incentive dropdown in the Configuration tab.
Mentorship intensity Low, Medium, High mentorship The mentorship intensity dropdown; also used in mentor cost and capacity calculations.
Delivery mode Fully in-person, blended, fully online The delivery mode dropdown in the Configuration tab.
Outbreak response capacity 30 days, 15 days, 7 days The outbreak response capacity dropdown in the Configuration tab (30, 15 or 7 days; default is 7 days).
Cost per trainee per month INR 75,000 – 400,000 The cost slider in the Configuration tab uses exactly this range.

All endorsement and perceived programme value results are derived from this mixed logit model. STEPS never extrapolates beyond these attribute levels.
