What is STEPS?

The Scalable Training Estimation and Planning System (STEPS) is a decision-support tool designed to assist the National Centre for Disease Control (NCDC) and partners in developing and testing scalable options for strengthening and expanding Field Epidemiology Training Programmes (FETP) in India.

STEPS combines:

  • Evidence from a discrete choice experiment (preference study) with potential FETP stakeholders;
  • Indicative epidemiological benefit assumptions for outbreak detection and response;
  • Simple cost and capacity assumptions, including opportunity cost of trainee time; and
  • Scenario comparison and export options for discussion in meetings and business case development.

How to use the tool

  1. Configure a scenario. Use the Configuration tab to set the programme tier, career incentives, mentorship intensity, delivery mode, response time, cohort size and number of cohorts.
  2. Review endorsement, value, costs and epidemiological benefits. Use the Results and Costing tabs to see endorsement, perceived programme value, costs, benefit–cost ratios and net epidemiological benefits for your scenario.
  3. Explore national implications and sensitivity. Use National simulation to scale up to national level and the Perceived programme value / Sensitivity tab for sensitivity checks.
  4. Save and export promising scenarios. Use the Saved scenarios tab to store and compare promising options and download them as Excel or PDF policy briefs.
  5. Adjust advanced assumptions as needed. Use Settings / Advanced to adjust epidemiological, value, cost and capacity assumptions (such as discount rates, outbreak values and mentor capacity).

Interpretation cautions

  • All values, costs and benefits are indicative and based on simplifying assumptions. They should be interpreted as orders of magnitude rather than precise estimates.
  • The perceived programme value measures are derived from a discrete choice experiment and represent an implicit valuation from stakeholders, not a literal budget or willingness to pay requirement.
  • Epidemiological benefits assume a link between FETP training, outbreak detection and response, which should be refined with better data over time.
  • Capacity and feasibility checks are high-level and depend critically on assumptions about available mentors, training sites and cohort sizes.

Summary of tabs and main outputs

Configuration

Define the FETP option: tier, incentives, mentorship intensity, delivery mode, response time, cohort size, number of cohorts and cost per trainee per month.

Results

View endorsement, perceived programme value, programme and mentor costs, benefit–cost ratios and net epidemiological benefits for the configured option.

Costing

See a detailed breakdown of programme and opportunity costs by cohort and nationally, including mentor support costs linked to mentorship intensity.

National simulation

Scale the scenario up nationally to see total costs, epidemiological benefits and perceived programme value for all cohorts and graduates.

Perceived programme value / Sensitivity

Explore how perceived programme value (from the preference study) and epidemiological benefits respond to changes in endorsement, costs, outbreak values and other assumptions.

Copilot briefing

Prepare combined prompts for Copilot or ChatGPT that summarise the current or shortlisted scenarios with assumptions and feasibility checks for rapid policy brief generation.

Saved scenarios

Save and compare strategic FETP options, shortlist the most promising scenarios, and export them to Excel or PDF policy briefs.

Settings / Advanced

Adjust epidemiological, value, discounting, currency, capacity and other assumptions, including mentor support costs and cross-sector benefit multipliers.

For sensitivity checks, use the National simulation and Perceived programme value / Sensitivity tabs to see how results change with cohorts, endorsement, outbreak values, and discounting assumptions.

Perceived programme value per trainee per month (from preference study) -

Implicit value that stakeholders place on each trainee-month of the configured programme, based on the preference study.

Total perceived programme value per cohort (from preference study) -

The estimated total perceived value stakeholders place on the programme for one cohort, not an actual budget.

Programme cost per cohort -

The direct cost of delivering the programme to one cohort, not including the cost of trainee time.

Total economic cost per cohort -

The economic cost of the programme per cohort, including opportunity cost of trainee time when enabled.

Total economic cost (all cohorts) -

The total economic cost across all cohorts planned with this configuration.

An indicative measure of the total perceived value of the programme across all cohorts based on the preference study.

Epidemiological benefits and benefit–cost ratio

Indicative epidemiological benefits per cohort (including cross-sector multiplier) on the assumptions used.

Total epidemiological benefit (all cohorts) -

Total indicative epidemiological benefits across all cohorts on the current assumptions.

Net epidemiological benefit per cohort -

This shows whether the health benefits from improved outbreak detection and response are greater than the costs for one cohort of trainees.

Benefit cost ratio (per cohort) -

Ratios above 1.0 indicate that estimated epidemiological benefits exceed costs on the assumptions used.

Interpret scenarios with Copilot and ChatGPT

This tab prepares a combined interpretation prompt so that Copilot or ChatGPT can produce a structured policy briefing. The STEPS tool never sends data automatically. You review the full text, copy it when you are ready and paste it into your chosen assistant in a separate browser tab.

How to use Copilot and ChatGPT with STEPS

Define and apply a scenario in STEPS, then save it in the Saved scenarios tab. When you click the buttons on this tab, STEPS refreshes the interpretation prompt with the latest configuration and shortlisted scenarios for Microsoft Copilot or ChatGPT.

Prepare and copy the interpretation prompt

When you click one of the buttons, STEPS refreshes the prompt using the latest configuration and scenarios, copies the full text to your clipboard and lets you paste it into Copilot or ChatGPT. If automatic copying is blocked, you can copy directly from the panel below and paste it into the new window.
