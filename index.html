Configure an FETP scenario

Choose tier, incentives, mentorship, delivery mode, and scale. Then apply the configuration to compute costs, epidemiological benefits and perceived programme value.

Frontline programmes are shorter and lower cost per trainee; advanced programmes are longer and more intensive.

Higher mentorship intensity improves quality and perceived value but increases mentor support costs and capacity requirements.

Shorter response times increase perceived value and epidemiological benefits.

When selected, the tool reports economic costs (direct plus opportunity cost). Direct costs are always reported separately.

The combination of cohorts and trainees is very large. Please check whether the scale is realistic given national capacity.

Costing details

This section makes the cost structure explicit, including mentor support costs and opportunity costs.

Unit and mentor support costs

This is the base mentor support cost per cohort. It is scaled using mentorship intensity multipliers (Low = 1.0, Medium = 1.3, High = 1.7).

Mentorship multiplier –

Determined by the selected mentorship intensity.

Cost breakdown per cohort

Programme cost per cohort (direct) –

Direct training and delivery cost per cohort, excluding mentor support and opportunity cost.

Mentor support cost per cohort –

Additional direct cost for mentor support for one cohort.

Direct cost per cohort –

Programme cost + mentor support cost per cohort.

Opportunity cost per cohort –

Only included in economic cost if opportunity cost toggle is ON.

Economic cost per cohort –

Direct cost + opportunity cost per cohort (if opportunity cost is included).
