STEPS – FETP India Decision Aid

STEPS helps explore options for strengthening and scaling up the Intermediate and Advanced Field Epidemiology Training Programmes (FETP) in India under NCDC’s stewardship.

It links a preference study (discrete choice experiment), costing assumptions and indicative epidemiological benefits to support discussions with NCDC, World Bank and other partners.

How to use STEPS

  1. Start on the Introduction and Attributes & levels tabs to understand what the preference model does and the key design features.
  2. Review Settings & assumptions and adjust the planning horizon, discount rate and value per outbreak if required.
  3. Use the Configuration tab to choose tier, incentives, mentorship intensity, delivery mode, cohorts, cohort size and cost per trainee per month.
  4. Click Apply configuration. The model from the preference study calculates endorsement, perceived programme value and indicative epidemiological benefits.
  5. Use Results, Costing details and the Perceived programme value & sensitivity tab to interpret the outputs.
  6. Use the National view tab to see cumulative costs and indicative outbreak benefits across all cohorts.
  7. Save one or more configurations as scenarios, then compare them and export using the Saved scenarios & exports tab.
  8. For narrative outputs, use the Copilot / ChatGPT briefing tab to generate a ready-to-paste prompt for Word, PowerPoint or policy briefs.

Important notes

  • All model-based results are indicative and depend on assumptions for costs and benefits. They should be interpreted as tools for structured discussion rather than precise forecasts.
  • The perceived programme value outputs come from a discrete choice experiment (preference study) and reflect relative valuations, not actual budgets or payment requirements.
  • The tool runs in this browser only. Saved scenarios and settings are not automatically shared or synced across devices.

Purpose of STEPS

STEPS is designed to support the National Centre for Disease Control (NCDC) and partners in exploring ways to strengthen and scale up the Intermediate and Advanced FETP in India.

It brings together three elements:

  1. A discrete choice experiment (preference study) capturing how stakeholders trade off programme design attributes.
  2. Costing templates that summarise direct financial costs of FETP training, including trainee time and programme delivery.
  3. An indicative epidemiological module translating graduates into outbreak responses and outbreak-related economic benefits.

STEPS does not replace detailed budgeting or epidemiological modelling. It is a structured decision aid to organise discussions on scenarios, assumptions and trade-offs.

How the preference study feeds into STEPS

The preference study used a discrete choice experiment to estimate a mixed logit model. This model quantifies how stakeholders trade off attributes such as tier, mentorship intensity, career incentives, delivery mode and cost per trainee per month.

In STEPS, this model is used to calculate:

  • Predicted endorsement (%) for a given configuration.
  • Predicted choice of the opt-out option (no change).
  • Perceived programme value (from the preference study) per trainee per month, per cohort and across all cohorts.

The perceived programme value is an implicit valuation derived from stated preferences in the discrete choice experiment. It is not an actual payment requirement or budget line.

Attributes and levels in the preference study

The discrete choice experiment included the following key attributes, with levels that map to STEPS configuration controls.

Attribute Levels Notes
Programme tier Frontline FETP, Intermediate FETP, Advanced FETP Controls duration, level of skills and target cadre.
Career incentive Government and partner certificate, University qualification, Government career pathway Represents formal recognition and progression opportunities.
Mentorship intensity Low, Medium, High Maps to indicative mentor-to-fellow ratios used in the capacity checks.
Delivery mode Blended, Fully in person, Fully online Determines relative travel and residential components.
Cost per trainee per month INR 75,000 – 400,000 Range used in the preference study; slider in configuration stays within this range.
Opt-out option Maintain current FETP arrangements Represents preference for the status quo if no new training option is appealing.

The mixed logit model uses these attributes and levels, along with the cost coefficient, to produce endorsement estimates and perceived value measures.

From coefficients to perceived programme value

Within the preference model, each attribute has an estimated coefficient. To derive approximate perceived programme value, attribute coefficients are scaled by the cost coefficient and expressed as an approximate rupee value per trainee per month.

These values should be interpreted as signalling how strongly stakeholders valued a given configuration in the experiment relative to cost, rather than as precise valuations or budget lines.

All coefficients and transformations used in STEPS are taken from the mixed logit model estimated on the preference study – no ad hoc or placeholder values are used.

Endorsement -

Choose opt out -

Perceived programme value and economic outcomes

Perceived programme value per trainee per month (INR) -

Derived from the preference study. Indicates relative strength of support per trainee, not an actual budget line.

Total perceived programme value per cohort (INR) -

The estimated total perceived programme value stakeholders place on the programme for one cohort, not an actual budget.

Direct cost per cohort (programme + mentor support) -

Direct cost of delivering the programme to one cohort, including mentor support but excluding opportunity cost of trainee time.

Total economic cost per cohort -

Adds opportunity cost of trainee time (if included) to the direct cost for one cohort.

Net outbreak-related economic benefit per cohort (INR) -

Difference between indicative outbreak-related economic benefits and total economic costs per cohort.

Benefit–cost ratio (outbreak benefits only) -

Comparison of outbreak benefits to costs for one cohort. Values above 1 mean benefits outweigh costs.

Epidemiological implications

Graduates per cohort (effective) -

Number of graduates from one cohort under the assumed completion rate.

Outbreak responses per year (national) -

Indicative number of outbreak responses per year across all cohorts and graduates.

Present value of outbreak-related benefits per cohort (INR, cross-sector adjusted) -

Present value of outbreak-related economic benefits for one cohort, adjusted by the cross-sector benefit multiplier to capture One Health and broader impacts.
