STEPS
Scalable Training Estimation and Planning System for FETP India
Technical appendix

What STEPS can do

STEPS provides a structured way to test FETP scale up options and communicate how different programme designs perform on endorsement, perceived programme value (from a preference study), costs and epidemiological outcomes.

  • Preferences and willingness to pay (WTP) information from a discrete choice experiment with stakeholders, summarised as a perceived programme value metric used in this tool.
  • Cost components and templates that combine programme cost categories for different tiers.
  • Simple epidemiological multipliers for outbreak response and health security benefits.
  • Scenario saving and export functions that support policy briefs and stakeholder meetings.

The perceived programme value metric in STEPS is an implicit valuation derived from the discrete choice experiment. It is not a budget line or payment requirement. It should be interpreted alongside cost and epidemiological assumptions, not as a literal payment expectation.

How STEPS works

  1. Review the Attributes and levels tab to see how tiers, incentives, mentorship and delivery mode are defined.
  2. Use the Configuration tab to define a scenario by selecting a tier, career incentive, mentorship structure, delivery mode, outbreak response time and cohort details.
  3. Click Apply configuration. The model from the preference study then calculates endorsement and perceived programme value metrics for the chosen design.
  4. Use View results summary to open a concise scenario summary.
  5. Save promising scenarios in the Saved scenarios tab and download them as Excel or a policy brief PDF for reporting and documentation.
  6. For sensitivity checks, use the National simulation and Perceived programme value / Sensitivity tabs to see how results change with cohorts, endorsement and benefit definitions, and use the Settings tab to adjust the key assumptions.

Perceived programme value from the preference study is the approximate rupee value stakeholders place on a configuration. Mixed logit is a regression model that allows these preference weights to vary across stakeholders.

Interpretation reminders

All endorsement and perceived programme value results in STEPS are based on a mixed logit preference model estimated from the preference study. The model predicts how likely stakeholders are to endorse different programme designs and how much implicit value they place on them.

Costs and epidemiological benefits in STEPS are indicative and depend on the assumptions in the Settings tab. Use them for comparative analysis, not as definitive budget or impact projections.

The preference study used a discrete choice experiment with the following programme attributes and levels. These are reflected in the options on the Configuration tab.

  • Frontline FETP
  • Intermediate FETP
  • Advanced FETP

Career incentive

  • Certificate only
  • Certificate plus university-recognised qualification
  • Structured career pathway

Mentorship structure

  • Low mentorship (5 or more fellows per mentor)
  • Medium mentorship (around 3–4 fellows per mentor)
  • High mentorship (2 fellows per mentor)

Teaching model

  • Blended (mix of online and in-person)
  • In-person only
  • Online only

Applied work and outbreak response

  • Average time to respond: 30 days
  • Average time to respond: 15 days
  • Average time to respond: 7 days

Programme cost to participants (per month)

  • ₹0 (no fees)
  • ₹1,000
  • ₹3,000

Other factors captured separately

  • Admission selectivity
  • Institutional setting and reputation
  • Stipends and allowances where applicable

Notes

The discrete choice experiment coefficients are combined into a mixed logit model with random parameters. In STEPS, these feed into the endorsement and perceived programme value indicators.

Settings and key assumptions

Use this panel to adjust core multipliers for this browser session. Changes feed into the Results, National simulation and Perceived programme value / Sensitivity tabs.

Use these settings when aligning STEPS with updated national estimates or partner discussions. You can change the indicative value per outbreak, the planning horizon, the discount rate for epidemiological benefits used in benefit calculations and the INR per USD rate for display.

Background

The University of Newcastle is developing a business case for strengthening and scaling FETP programmes in India. STEPS is one of the tools in this work. It provides a structured way to test scale up options and explain cost, perceived programme value and epidemiological implications in policy discussions.

For full details, including model specification, estimation outputs, formulae and worked examples, refer to the technical appendix. This notice explains how STEPS uses perceived programme value information derived from the preference study in combination with cost and epidemiological assumptions.

Endorsement and opt out

Endorsement rate -

Predicted share of stakeholders who endorse the configured FETP option.

Opt out rate -

Predicted share of stakeholders who prefer not to fund additional FETP training under this configuration.

Perceived programme value and economic outcomes

Perceived programme value per trainee per month (preference study) -

The estimated monthly perceived programme value per trainee from the preference study. Higher values indicate stronger relative attractiveness of this configuration.

Total perceived programme value per cohort (preference study) -

The estimated total perceived programme value for one cohort, based on the preference study. This helps compare programme design options but is not a literal payment expectation.

Programme cost per cohort -

The direct cost of delivering the programme to one cohort, not including the cost of trainee time.

Total economic cost per cohort -

Programme cost plus opportunity cost of trainee time where applicable. This is the main cost figure used in benefit–cost ratios.

Net benefit per cohort (epidemiological) -

Net epidemiological benefit per cohort, based on the outbreak response and health security assumptions in the model.

Epidemiological implications

Outbreak responses per year -

Approximate number of outbreak responses per year once all graduates are in post under this configuration.

Indicative epidemiological benefit per cohort -

An estimate of the value of better outbreak detection and response from one group of trainees, based on assumptions in the model.

Capacity and feasibility checks

Required mentors per cohort -

Calculated from trainees per cohort and mentorship intensity assumptions.

Required mentors nationally -

Required mentors for all cohorts for this configuration.

Available mentors nationally -

Pulled from the Settings tab (available mentors nationally).

Mentor shortfall (if any) -

Positive values indicate additional mentors needed to deliver this configuration.

Available training sites / hubs -

Optional: available FETP training sites or hubs in the system.

Implied cohorts per site per year -

Simple ratio of total cohorts to available sites, compared with the max cohorts per site setting.

Feasibility status -

Summary judgement comparing required and available capacity, highlighting any mentor or site gaps.

Cost structure and sources

This is the base mentor support cost per cohort. The tool applies simple multipliers for low, medium and high mentorship intensity.

Cost templates combine salary, training, travel, supervision and other categories for each tier. You can adjust templates or supply your own in the technical appendix version of STEPS.

Interpret scenarios with Microsoft Copilot and ChatGPT

This tab prepares an interpretation prompt that summarises the current configuration, endorsement, perceived programme value, cost and epidemiological outcomes, feasibility checks and key assumptions. You can paste the prompt into Microsoft Copilot or ChatGPT to generate a policy-ready narrative.

The prompt includes the current scenario values, any shortlisted scenarios, key assumptions, capacity checks and a reminder to keep language policy-ready and to avoid technical jargon. You can edit the prompt manually before copying it.
